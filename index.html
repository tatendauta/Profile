How I Work

I believe product analysts deliver the most value when they move beyond standard reporting to become strategic decision partners.

1. Start with the Business Problem

I avoid jumping straight into query blocks and raw spreadsheets. My first step is always to clarify and frame the underlying business goal. Understanding "Why is funding down?" allows me to design studies that address actual issues rather than superficial symptoms.

2. Make Metrics Trustworthy

If stakeholders don't trust the numbers, the data is useless. I focus on establishing clean, consistent metric dictionaries, cleaning up reporting lag, and mapping legacy models directly to streaming systems. This builds complete confidence in key decisions.

3. Bring Stakeholders Along Early

Data findings shouldn't be delivered as a surprising, last-minute presentation. I loop in product, design, compliance, and engineering partners early on, validating assumptions and incorporating their practical insights before finalizing any strategic recommendations.

4. Measure Outcomes, Not Outputs

The value of an analyst isn't measured by how many SQL scripts they write or dashboards they build. True success lies in the business metrics influenced, the conversion bottlenecks resolved, the engineering hours saved, and the strategic decisions empowered.
